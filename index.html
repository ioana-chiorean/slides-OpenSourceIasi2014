How metrics motivate people

"There has never been a more important time for the recognition industry to make the connection between business objectives and employee recognition programs, and metrics are critical to proving that connection.” (Jennifer Lumba)

@ioana_cis #fcm2

Ioana Chiorean, FLOSS Community Metrics meeting, January 30th 2015

Why metrics can help more…

  • Cash was (is) the king - payday
  • End of year bonus
  • ..
Cash is king, we've all heard, and certainly just about everyone has experienced the motivating effects of a promised . So what could possibly be wrong with using financial carrots to encourage your employees to work to their full capacity?

Are those wrong?

  • No. But..
  • They will lack agility
  • They will look for time depending bonus more than performance
  • .. because companies must focus on the recognition not the rewards, reinforcing desired behaviors that contribute to positive business results

Wait whaat?

  • Performance-based recognition program aka metrics
  • + compensation package aka rewards
  • = succesful stories (lower cost to improve the performance )
A performance-based recognition program, coupled with a compensation package, drives the behavior required to align employees with corporate goals. Through metrics encouraged by timely performance-based recognition, employees understand how their contributions affect the bottom line.

Why?…

  • Community members/ emplyees need to
  • - feel they are part of the success
  • - their work made an impact

How they help:

Measure the Correct Things

  • Count what it counts
  • Give them the driver seat
  • But ask them to use the security belt
  • Draw them
  • Gamification
“It’s important to measure specific goals that will contribute to your organization’s success. What does the big picture look like and how do we get there? Set specific goals" Have them set their Own Metrics “Managers should ask employees to come up with specific metrics, which will hit targets to drive results. If you empower employees to be in the driver’s seat and make decisions that affect their success, then they are more likely to be ambitious and set stretch goals that push their limits. When employees feel like they are in control and have management support, they are likely to be engaged and hit targets based on specific metrics." “I'll sit down with my direct reports and say, what are the things that we should be measuring to ensure that we're on track to hit our goals?" "If you hire great people and you have a strong culture where people believe in the vision of the company and feel like they have the empowerment to help the company achieve that vision, actually people will likely set targets that are too high because they're ambitious and they want to succeed." driver's seat when it comes to their metrics. ensuring employees don't set unrealistic expectations for themselves Make them simple, understandable - > draw them - easier to understand

Review and Reward

  • Status of progress
  • Status of relevancy
  • Know how to share the experience
  • Public recognition - best - try to top it
  • .. but keep it a healthy competition
  • Frustation - they do things and no one acknowledges it
check progress often – daily, weekly, or monthly at the latest. By the time the quarter ends, it will be too late to salvage losses resulting from a lack of follow-up." "Public recognition is crucial when it comes to reviewing metrics and rewarding performance. If you want employees to exhibit behaviors required to drive results, then it is necessary to publicly recognize these behaviors in order to drive engagement. Metrics are a great tool to measure progress – so if you publicly reward metrics, employees will eventually achieve the end goal."

Results:

  • MOTIVATED people
  • Real recognition - lower costs
  • Improve organizational performance
  • .. but also identify who isn’t rowing with the team.
